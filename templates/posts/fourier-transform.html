Fourier Transform

The Fourier Transform of x(t)

Inverse Fourier Transform of X(f)

One of the main purposes of the Fourier Transform is to point out the dominant frequencies of a waveform. This can easily be seen with the FT of a regular sine or cosine wave.

The peaks of the FT are locate near -1 and 1 Hz which indicate these are the dominant frequencies in x(t). This makes sense since the frequency of x(t) is 1 Hz. The Inverse Fourier Transform can also be used to reconstruct waves from the calculated FT.

Note: The reason the wave does not look exactly like the original is because I integrated over a finite number of frequencies rather than from minus infinity to infinity. If I increased the range of frequencies, the reconstructed wave would look more like the original.
